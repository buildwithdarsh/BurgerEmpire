## A2. Autopsy: What Killed October's "Peak"?

October 2025 (3,441 orders) was the highest single month in BB's history. It looked like a recovery. It was a trap.

**The discount budget tells the real story:**

| Month | Orders | Discount Spend | Discount/Order |
|---|---|---|---|
| Jan '25 | 2,943 | ~Rs.68K | Rs.23.1 |
| Mar '25 | 3,299 | ~Rs.82K | Rs.24.9 |
| Jun '25 | 2,931 | ~Rs.74K | Rs.25.2 |
| **Oct '25** | **3,441** | **Rs.1,25,773** | **Rs.36.6** |
| Feb '26 | 1,545 | Rs.21,017 | **Rs.13.6** |

October's per-order discount was **58% higher** than the annual average. This is the signature of Zomato's discount-gamed ranking algorithm at work:

**How it works:**
1. High discount offers → higher conversion rate on listings
2. Higher conversion rate → Zomato algorithm rewards with better placement
3. Better placement → more impressions → more orders (appears organic)
4. When discount budget is cut → conversion drops → ranking drops → impressions drop
5. The "peak" was entirely manufactured and non-repeatable

**Proof:** February 2026's discount % dropped to 4.6% (from 12.5%) — the lowest in 14 months. Zomato orders simultaneously hit 154 — a 72.1% YoY collapse. Perfect inverse correlation.
