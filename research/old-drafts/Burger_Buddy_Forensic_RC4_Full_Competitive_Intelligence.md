## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**The market BB entered in 2018 no longer exists.**

When Burger Empire launched at City Center, Patel Nagar, it operated in a Gwalior with no national burger chains, no well-funded local competitors, and no Zomato Visibility Plan to outspend. That market had approximately 10–20 informal burger stalls and zero institutional fast-food competition in the burger category.

By March 2026, the same city has **~117 registered burger-serving establishments** across four competitive tiers: 3 national chains (5 outlets), 3 regional franchise brands, 5 local multi-outlet brands, and an estimated 100+ single-outlet and informal operators. BB's 8 outlets represent ~7% of registered listings.

BB's sales fell 47.8% (orders) and 34.6% (revenue) year-on-year in February 2026 — not because demand for burgers collapsed, but because the **competitive supply exploded** around BB at the exact moment it was weakened by the CC/137/2024 legal verdict, platform algorithm changes, and the absence of any direct digital channel.

## SECTION 7: THE SUPPLY SHOCK ANALYSIS — WHY 117 OUTLETS EXPLAINS THE COLLAPSE

**The central paradox:** BB's sales fell 47.8% orders / 34.6% revenue YoY in Feb 2026 while the total number of burger establishments in Gwalior grew to ~117.

This is not a paradox — it is a textbook supply shock.

When BK, KFC, and McD entered Gwalior (2022–2024), they did three things simultaneously:

**1. They grew the burger category** — National advertising legitimized "eating a burger" as a Gwalior dining occasion, expanding total market demand.

**2. They captured the aspiration tier** — Students, young professionals, and families who previously had no accessible national-brand burger experience now had BK/KFC/McD as their default choice for "special occasion burgers." BB lost its position as Gwalior's premium burger option to brands with better ambience, apps, and loyalty.

**3. They triggered local entrepreneur entry** — The national chain presence signalled to 30–40 local entrepreneurs that burger-selling was a viable business in Gwalior. The result: Burger Budds, Boxo Burger, Burger Point, Burger Queen, Burger Bytes all entered between approximately 2022 and 2025.

BB in 2026 is caught in a three-layer squeeze:
- **Above (aspirational):** BK/KFC/McD with apps, malls, Rs.99 combos — BB cannot compete at this level
- **Alongside (parity):** Burger Budds, Boxo Burger, Burger Point at the same Rs.73–350 price point — with no legal controversy and active Zomato listings
- **Below (price):** 80–100 street-adjacent operators at Rs.40–80 per burger — capturing the economy segment BB historically served

And BB brought the CC/137/2024 verdict (permanently indexed on Google via LiveLaw and Free Press Journal), a collapsed Zomato algorithm ranking (from stopping discounts in November 2025), and zero alternative channels to this three-front price war.

## SECTION 10: STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS — WHAT BB MUST DO GIVEN THIS LANDSCAPE

### Defend First (48 hours–2 weeks)

**Priority 1 — Mahalgaon:** BB's 4.3–4.5 rated outlet is under attack from Boxo Burger (same zone) and Chaatizza. This is BB's strongest outlet and the anchor for recovery. Launch stamp card loyalty immediately at this outlet. Negotiate with Magicpin for featured placement (free activation available). Do not let Boxo Burger establish a review base that rivals BB's.

The goal is to push the review count to 20,000+ to create a gap no new entrant can close in 12 months.

**Priority 3 — Kill the CC/137/2024 Google presence:** Five food bloggers posting positive, recent content about BB Gwalior will push the LiveLaw and FPJ articles from page 1 of Google results. This is doable in 30 days at Rs.25,000–50,000 blogger fee total.
