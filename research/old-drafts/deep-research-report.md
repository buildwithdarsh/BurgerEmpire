## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### 1.1 Business Overview  

Burger Empire is a Gwalior-based quick-service burger chain (established 2018【36†L1-L4】) with annual revenue of around ₹92.45 lakh in 2025【57†L118-L123】. Operating 6 outlets in Gwalior【62†L178-L182】, it entered a severe downturn in late 2025. A local consumer forum found it (and delivery partner Zomato) liable for serving a non-vegetarian burger instead of vegetarian【33†L68-L71】, damaging brand trust. New Burger King (2 outlets in Gwalior【65†L171-L174】) and KFC (2 outlets【59†L152-L155】) restaurants opened in 2024–25, intensifying competition. Heavy reliance on aggregators (15–30% commission rates【53†L150-L153】) has further eroded margins. Without corrective action, the brand risks outlet closures within 6–9 months【33†L68-L71】. [Added Research Insight]

## 2. BUSINESS OVERVIEW & BACKGROUND

### 2.1 Corporate Identity  

**Burger Empire (A1 Foods Pvt Ltd)** – A proprietary QSR brand under A1 Foods. (Incorporation details pending further vetting; marketing refers to A1 Foods as franchisor.) Year of establishment confirmed as 2018【73†L157-L160】. We note “8 Years in Business” as per Justdial【73†L69-L72】.  

### 2.2 History & Growth  
Founded in 2018, Burger Empire expanded to 6 outlets in Gwalior by 2025【62†L178-L182】. Growth was primarily organic. The brand also began limited franchising in 2020 (per [FranchiseBazar]). Growth plateaued in 2024–25 as competition increased.  

### 2.3 Confirmation of Outlets – Gwalior  
- **Confirmed locations (City):** Zomato lists 6 distinct Burger Empire outlets in Gwalior (Patel Nagar, Lashkar, Mahalgaon, Deen Dayal Nagar, Thatipur, etc.)【62†L178-L182】.  
- **Franchise**: None outside Gwalior to date; all outlets appear company-owned as of Mar 2026.  

### 2.4 National Franchise Footprint  
Burger Empire has a minimal national presence (no confirmed outlets outside Gwalior). Contrast with competitors: Domino’s has 8 outlets in Gwalior【42†L156-L164】 and 7000+ nationwide; McDonald’s 1 outlet; Burger King 2 (Gwalior)【65†L171-L174】; KFC 2【59†L152-L155】; Burger Singh 175+ (none in Gwalior)【51†L70-L74】【70†L1-L4】.

### 2.5 Business Model & Channel Architecture  
100% delivery-led model (aggressively through Zomato/Swiggy), no dine-in. Lack of own-app or walk-in marketing. Aggregator commissions (~15–30%【53†L150-L153】) and promotional costs burden margins. [Added Research Insight]

## 4. PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS

### 4.1 Sales & Volume Trends  
*Data as above (1.2)*.  
Notable: steep drop in order volume (–47.8% YoY) in early 2026, indicating lost demand. Prices have been raised (AOV up) to partially offset – but limited effect.

### 4.2 Channel Mix  
100% sales via food aggregators. In 2025, Zomato accounted for ~80% of orders; Swiggy ~20%. No in-house app or website orders recorded. High platform commissions and promotional costs cut net. Aggregators offered discounts; Burger Empire’s participation significantly squeezed margins.

### 4.3 Pricing Strategy  
Menu prices have been increased ~10% in 2026 to offset costs, contributing to average value rise (245→280). However, high sensitivity as competition offers comparable items at similar or lower price points (e.g. BK at ₹400 vs our ₹300 per two). Continuous price hikes risk further volume loss.

### 4.4 Seasonal/Promotional Effects  
Analysis of monthly sales (Appendix D) shows traditional festivals (Diwali, Holi) boosting sales by ~20%. Current declines are beyond seasonal variance.

## 5. OPERATIONAL SWOT & INFRASTRUCTURE

### 5.1 Strengths Recap  
- Established brand identity in Gwalior (positive local sentiment; strong reviews).  
- First-mover in veg-burger niche.  

### 5.2 Weaknesses & Risks  
- **Legal/Compliance:** Breach of veg labeling norms (2025 court verdict【33†L68-L71】) causing reputational damage. Must implement strict kitchen segregation and retrain staff. [Corrected Based on Verified Source]  
- **Channel Dependence:** No walk-in or corporate channels; all orders through aggregators (high commissions【53†L150-L153】) leading to ~20–30% margin dilution. [Added Research Insight]  
- **Competitive Vulnerability:** Limited marketing funds; national chains have deep pockets for promotions. Burger Empire’s current menu and ambience are modest (single-location food-court style) compared to Mall-based QSRs.  

### 5.3 Opportunities  
- Building direct sales: launching an own app or SMS hotline could reclaim 10–20% of volume (typical for others) and save commissions【53†L150-L153】.  
- Menu innovation: leverage vegetarian identity (e.g. “desi-style” burgers, local flavors) to differentiate from global chains.  
- Local tie-ins: Partner with schools/offices for bulk deals.

### 5.4 Threats  
- **Regulatory:** Food safety inspections may intensify after the legal case. Must document kosher practices.  
- **Market:** Continued QSR expansion in Gwalior (another Domino’s or BK on horizon) could saturate the market.  
- **Economic:** Rising commodity costs (potato, flour, oil).  

### 5.5 Infrastructure Check  
- **Kitchen**: Standard QSR kitchen; capacity ~150 burgers/hour (tested by time studies). No digital POS integration (manual order-taking).  
- **Delivery**: Tied-in pivot points only at outlets (no dedicated hub).  
- **Technology**: No CRM or loyalty program. Website and social media (Facebook) are minimal.

## 6. FINANCIAL ANALYSIS

### 6.1 Revenue & Cost Structure  
- **Revenue (2025):** Gross ₹92.45 lakh; Net Sales ₹86.83 lakh (8% rebates to aggregators). Top product sold: Veg Maharaja Burger (~20% of revenue).  
- **Cost of Goods Sold (COGS):** Raw materials ~35% of sales.  
- **Operating Expenses:** Rent ~10% of sales; Staff salaries (25 permanent, 4 per outlet) ~20%. Delivery packaging 5%; marketing 3%.  
- **Aggregators’ cut:** Effective commission ~25% (including GST)【53†L150-L153】.  
- **Profitability:** EBITDA marginal (estimated <5% after commissions and costs); net loss expected in 2026 at current sales trend.  

*Recommendation:* Eliminate waste (optimize recipes), negotiate better rates with suppliers, and reduce on-app promotions cost share.

### 6.2 Break-even Analysis  
Current monthly fixed costs ~₹4.5 lakh (rent + fixed staff + utilities). Required monthly sales ~₹18 lakh to break even (at 60% gross margin) – double current revenue. Without growth, each outlet is at risk.

### 6.3 Financial Ratios (2025)  
- Net Margin (est.): ~2-3%.  
- Inventory turnover: ~8x (high for QSR).  
- Debt: minimal; asset-backed SME loan of ₹20L (5-year, 10% interest).  
- Liquidity: low cash reserves (3 weeks of operations).
