## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY — WHAT THE FULL SCAN REVEALS

This is not a comfortable document. The digital footprint of Burger Empire in March 2026 tells a story of a brand in operational distress trying to hire its way out. The job postings — Video Editor at Rs.10K–20K, Kitchen Staff (fresher/experienced both welcome), Cleaning Help (paid sick time listed as a perk, which signals staff turnover awareness), Store Manager across multiple outlets — are being posted simultaneously across 4 platforms during the exact period when Zomato orders are down 72% year-on-year.

The momo addition is particularly alarming because:
1. Momos require a steamer (for regular) or deep fryer (for fried) + dedicated prep workflow
2. Szechuan gravy requires a wok station and Chinese sauce base
3. These are not QSR adjuncts — they are a distinct cuisine with distinct equipment
4. Momos are dominated by dedicated momo chains and street vendors in Gwalior who produce them faster and cheaper
5. Adding momos in the same period that burger orders are collapsing is brand entropy in real-time

## SECTION 9: COMPETITIVE HIRING INTELLIGENCE — WHAT THE JOB MARKET REVEALS

The fact that BB is simultaneously hiring for:
- Video Editor (content)
- Kitchen Staff (operations)
- Cleaning Help (facilities)
- Store Manager (management)
- Restaurant Manager (management)

...across 4 platforms (Indeed, Glassdoor, Internshala, LinkedIn via Shubham Tikekar)...

...in the same quarter that Zomato orders fell 72%...

...tells a very specific operational story.

**Kitchen Staff + Cleaning (Fresher):** High turnover at outlet level. The explicit "fresher welcome" signals that experienced staff are leaving or not applying. Rs.10K–12K/month for kitchen roles in Gwalior is competitive but not exceptional — the food provided perk and paid sick time listings suggest BB is trying to differentiate on non-cash benefits.

**Store Manager (Govindpuri):** A new area not previously in the outlet census. BB may be planning a new outlet in Govindpuri despite the crisis — or this is a replacement hire for a manager who left.

**Restaurant Manager:** Multi-outlet supervisory role. The Shubham Tikekar LinkedIn post specifically says "restaurant managers and kitchen @ location Gwalior" — plural management roles across the Gwalior cluster.

**Video Editor (Rs.10K–20K, Hybrid):** This is the most strategically interesting hire. The description ("edit reels and be smart") suggests BB has decided that content marketing is a recovery lever. This is not wrong — Instagram Reels for local food brands in India generate measurable order lift. But Rs.10,000–20,000/month for a hybrid video editor in 2026 is the low end of market. A skilled reel creator in Gwalior would expect Rs.18K–30K. At Rs.10K, BB will attract a beginner. The content quality will reflect that, and in a post-CC/137/2024 environment where brand trust is the primary problem, poor-quality content can make things worse.

## SECTION 10: THE DIGITAL FOOTPRINT VERDICT

After scanning every accessible platform, the picture that emerges is of a brand that has:

**Built correctly:**
- Strong Zomato review base (15,800+ reviews is genuinely difficult to build; it took years)
- Physical outlet footprint across Gwalior's key zones
- A hero SKU (Aloo Tikki Twist) that customers independently seek out
- Founder-run energy that creates personalised service culture
- Independent quality rankings (#2 fast food in Gwalior, ThreeBestRated)

**Neglected entirely:**
- ONDC registration (free, 3–5 days, Rs.8–12L annual recovery potential)
- WhatsApp Business ordering (Rs.40–50K setup, zero commission)
- Loyalty programme (zero retention mechanism for 15,800+ Zomato reviewers)
- Magicpin activation (free visibility in a platform with 4,500+ Mahalgaon reviews)
- LinkedIn company page claim (free, takes 10 minutes)
- Merging duplicate Facebook pages (free)
- Zomato Visibility Plan subscription (Rs.6,500/month per outlet)

**Made actively worse:**
- Menu expansion into momos while burger orders collapsed
- Video Editor hire at below-market pay with below-professional JD
- Franchise pitch materials that omit CC/137/2024 context
- Ongoing Veg Chilli Loaded Burger on menu (1,331 post-verdict orders)
- Co-founder geographically distant from operational crisis (Rahul Saraogi based in UP)

**The gap is not between what BB has and what it needs. The gap is between what BB has and what it is using.**

6,500 Instagram followers with zero WhatsApp conversion link = 6,500 people who can't order without going through Zomato at 25% commission. 15,800+ Zomato reviews with zero stamp card or loyalty token = 15,800+ transactions with no retention mechanism. A technical co-founder (BTech CS) with no digital ordering channel = expertise without application.

The digital footprint scan makes the same diagnosis as the POS forensics, the competitive analysis, and the menu data: the crisis is recoverable, the assets exist, and the decisions that would fix it are not being made.
