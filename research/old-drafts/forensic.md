## PART 1: THE KEY LEGAL FINDING — ZOMATO'S OWN CONTRACT CLAUSES

Research into Zomato's official published merchant agreements revealed the precise contractual timelines that governed what happened to Burger Empire after the March 20, 2025 verdict.

### Clause A — Breach & Suspension (14-Day Window)
**Source:** Zomato Pay Terms & Conditions — Official Zomato Policy Page
**URL:** https://www.zomato.com/policies/zomato-pay/

> *"Either Party may terminate the Form and the Terms by issuing a **45 (forty-five) days** prior written notice of termination to the other Party. Notwithstanding anything to the contrary, Zomato may **forthwith terminate the agreement or suspend the Restaurant** if the Merchant is in breach of these Terms, which, in the case of a breach **capable of remedy**, is not remedied **within 14 days** after intimation is given to the Merchant specifying the default."*

**What this means for BB:** From the date Zomato formally notified BB of the breach (likely within days of the March 20 verdict being published nationally), BB had only **14 days to remedy** — provide kitchen segregation evidence, apology, corrective action. BB's total silence meant Zomato had full contractual authority to **suspend BB immediately** after April 3, 2025.

---

### Clause B — Commission/Fee Change Notice (45-Day Window)
**Source:** Zomato Dining Terms & Conditions (last updated Aug 09, 2024) — Official Zomato Policy
**URL:** https://www.zomato.com/policies/zomato-dining/

> *"Zomato shall communicate any change to Commission rates or additional charges/fee via email or any other mode of communication to the Merchant with a **prior intimation of forty-five (45) days**."*

**What this means for BB:** The long-distance delivery fee (Rs.20–40/order) that hit BB in June 2025 would have been formally notified to BB ~mid-April 2025 — exactly when the breach window was expiring. BB received legal AND commercial bad news in the same 2-week window.

---

### Clause C — Court Payment Deadline (45 Days)
**Source:** Law Trend — Full case report CC/137/2024
**URL:** https://lawtrend.in/consumer-court-directs-zomato-to-compensate-customer-for-incorrect-delivery-of-non-vegetarian-food/

> *"The compensation amounts were to be paid within **45 days** from the date of the order (March 20, 2025)."*

**Deadline:** May 4, 2025
**What this means:** If BB failed to pay by May 4, 2025, complainant Ashish Sharma (through Advocate Aditya Sharma) could file an **Execution Petition** keeping the case legally "live" for another 2–4 months. This would extend Zomato's compliance review period through July–September 2025.

## PART 5: WHY OCTOBER WAS A FAKE PEAK

This is the most misunderstood point in the data. October 2025 looks like recovery — but it was the opposite:

| Month | Orders | Discount Spend | Discount % | Zomato Orders |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sep '25 | 3,158 | ~Rs.1.05L | 11.8% | 848 |
| **Oct '25** | **3,441** | **Rs.1,25,773** | **12.5%** | **989** |
| Nov '25 | 2,554 | Rs.96,406 | 12.2% | 670 |
| Dec '25 | 2,259 | ~Rs.79K | 12.3% | 566 |
| Feb '26 | 1,545 | Rs.21,017 | **4.6%** | **154** |

**What happened:** BB pushed its highest-ever discount budget in October, which temporarily manipulated Zomato's algorithm (higher conversion rate → better ranking → more organic impressions). When that budget ran out in November, the platform snapped back to its "true" algorithmic position — which was deeply demoted. The cliff was so sharp because the October peak was entirely artificial.

**Key source confirming algorithmic discount mechanics:**
> Restaurant Coach (Nov 2025): *"Platforms are now charging different commission rates based on factors like your restaurant's location, order volume... Paid Priority Listing: A new feature gives restaurants higher visibility in search results — for an additional fee. This creates a two-tier system where restaurants that pay more get seen more."*
> **URL:** https://restaurantcoach.in/zomato-and-swiggy-announce-new-commission-structures-and-priority-listing-features-for-restaurants/
